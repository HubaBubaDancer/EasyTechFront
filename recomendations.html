Can beginners build a PC? ▶
Building a computer is surprisingly easy. You'll only need a few tools, a good level of understanding of the parts, and the ability to follow some simple instructions. If you can build ready-to-assemble furniture, you'll be able to build your own PC! PC building is infinitely varied, so no guide can cover every scenario. But we'll cover the most common issues you'll face assembling a desktop in 2024. You may decide to go fancier or more budget-focused with your build, but you'll walk away with a great grounding in what you need to know to do the job with confidence. Gathering the right tools is essential. You'll typically need screwdrivers (usually Phillips-head), an anti-static wrist strap (optional but recommended), and thermal paste, which is often pre-applied to CPU coolers but can be bought separately. When you're ready to build, prepare your workspace to be clean, well-lit, and static-free. Start by installing the CPU into the motherboard socket and securing it. Next, attach the CPU cooler, applying thermal paste if it isn't pre-applied. Insert the RAM sticks into the designated slots on the motherboard. Then, secure the motherboard to the case using screws. Attach SSDs or HDDs to the case and connect them to the motherboard. Next, install the power supply by securing it in the case and connecting the necessary power cables to the motherboard, CPU, GPU, and storage devices. Insert the GPU into the PCIe slot on the motherboard and secure it. Finally, connect the case’s front panel connectors, such as the power button, USB ports, and audio jacks, to the motherboard. Once everything is assembled, check all connections to ensure they are properly secured. Turn on the PC and enter the BIOS/UEFI to check if all components are recognized. Install your chosen operating system, such as Windows or Linux, and update drivers for the motherboard, GPU, and other components. Many online tutorials, including videos, guide you step-by-step through the process. Component manuals provide detailed installation instructions, and forums and communities like Reddit, Tom's Hardware, and Linus Tech Tips offer valuable advice and support. By carefully following these steps, beginners can successfully build a PC. It's a rewarding experience that can also teach valuable skills about computer hardware.
What are the 3 most important components of any computer system? ▶
The CPU, GPU, and RAM are considered the most important components of a computer system. Central Processing Unit (CPU): Function: The CPU, often referred to as the "brain" of the computer, is responsible for executing instructions from software and performing calculations. It processes data and controls the flow of information within the system. Importance: The performance of a computer largely depends on the capabilities of its CPU. A faster CPU can handle more tasks simultaneously and execute them more quickly. Memory (RAM): Function: Random Access Memory (RAM) temporarily stores data that the CPU needs to access quickly while performing tasks. It allows for fast read and write access to a storage medium that the CPU can use efficiently. Importance: Sufficient RAM is crucial for multitasking and running applications smoothly. More RAM allows a computer to handle more tasks at once and access data more quickly than if it had to read from slower storage like a hard drive or SSD. Storage (Hard Drive or SSD): Function: Storage devices, such as Hard Disk Drives (HDDs) or Solid State Drives (SSDs), store all the data on a computer, including the operating system, applications, and user files. SSDs are faster and more reliable than HDDs. Importance: Adequate and efficient storage is essential for saving data and ensuring quick access to it. An SSD can significantly improve the overall speed and performance of a computer compared to an HDD, especially in tasks that involve reading and writing large amounts of data. These components work together to ensure a computer operates efficiently and effectively, allowing users to perform a wide range of tasks from basic computing to complex processing.

Top 10 mistakes when choosing PC components ▶
If you’re looking to build a new PC system, there are a lot of things to think about. From start to finish everything needs to be considered; choosing your components, assembling the PC, and installing programmes once the build is up and running. Whether this is your first PC build, or you’ve done it before, this article will walk you through the top 10 PC build mistakes that people usually make. Avoid these mistakes and your PC build process will be a lot easier! Let’s check them out. 1. Buying a low-cost and poor-quality power supply If you’re building a new PC one of the most talked about mistakes is buying a poor-quality power supply. When you get to the stage of shopping for this component, you should already have a rough idea on how much power the rest of your hardware will need. Always purchase slightly more wattage than you are estimating so overtime it won’t affect your system. There’s also a grading system to help you shop, this is called 80plus. The grades range from Plus-to-Plus Titanium, what you get will depend on what you need and your budget. Don’t worry, there are affordable power supply units on the market but always check whether it’s powerful enough for your machine. 2. Purchasing components that aren’t compatible with one another One of the most common mistakes when building a PC is purchasing components that are not compatible with each other. There are a few ways that you can find out whether your components are compatible. The easiest way is to add all of your desired parts into PCPartPicker* and the tool will identify if there are any compatibility issues. If you’re purchasing Kingston products, you can find our PC compatibility search on our site or contact customer service to speak with someone who can help. 3. Forgetting to install the I/O shield An I/O shield is a thin removable metal plate that provides casing for all the input and output on the back of a PC. A common mistake is forgetting to insert this before the motherboard and other components are placed in the casing. Remember this in order to avoid the pain of having to take apart your PC build. 4. Not adding in the motherboard stand-offs Your PC case has all the screw holes necessary to mount your motherboard into the right position. Remember to add the standoffs and risers as they will ensure that your motherboard is slightly raised from the casing which prevents the bottom of the motherboard’s solder points and circuit traces from shorting out against the metal case. Safety first! 5. Installing the fans the wrong way Cooling your system is very important when running a PC. Installing fans helps to remove all the hot air from the casing so that your build is always at a healthy temperature. However, it can be an easy mistake to install the fans the wrong way. If you’ve purchased a case with fans already installed then this shouldn’t be a problem, but if you need to add fans to your system make sure they’re positioned the right way so the air flows in the correct direction. Check out our memory installation guide. 6. CPU installation Some beginners will not know how to place the CPU into the slot. Installing your CPU incorrectly could potentially damage or snap the pins, depending on how much force is used. A CPU will always have markings on one corner that should match one on the board to show you the correct way to install it. 7. Applying too little or too much thermal paste Applying the wrong amount of thermal paste can result in poor CPU and system performance. Thermal paste provides heat transfer between the CPU and the heatsink. If little paste is applied this will lead to poor cooling of the CPU and increase its temperature, which can lead to long-term damage. 8. Installing memory improperly If you have an even number of memory modules, you should always have them spaced apart. Your motherboards manual will have information on which slots your memory modules need to slot into. Also, check if your gaming memory is installed correctly, you will hear a click when it’s inserted the right With the introduction of DDR5, you’re currently unable to mix different capacities in channels or banks. If you already have a kit of 2x8GB you won’t be able to add an additional set of 2x16GB in retrospect. Always buy the correct capacity for your needs when considering a DDR5 build. Some DDR5 motherboards may receive a BIOS update in the future to allow for more flexibility in memory capacity combinations so check with the manufacturer. 9. Plugging cables into the wrong ports This mistake can be easily avoided if you check all the cables before going further. All cables need to be connected and in the right input otherwise your system will not turn on and you could run the risk of damaging the parts. Plug-in locations can always be found in the manual. 10. Last but not least - read the manuals! The manuals are pivotal for any successful PC build. If you want to create a pristine system with no errors, then a manual will be your best friend. While there is no guarantee you won’t make any mistakes during your first or even second PC build; after these points, there is a much better chance that you’ll be able to dodge these common errors.
